# The Follow-Up Email Nobody Sends

**Subject line:** The follow-up email that wins deals (and almost nobody sends it)

---

After a sales call, most reps send some version of "Great chatting today! Let me know if you have any questions." It's polite. It's forgettable. And it does absolutely nothing to move the deal forward.

The email that actually closes deals is the one with action items, owners, and dates. The accountability email.

**The insight:** The best closers I've talked to all do the same thing: they send a recap that reads like meeting minutes. "Here's what we discussed. Here's what you agreed to do by Friday. Here's what I'm handling by Wednesday. Here's the next meeting." It sounds basic. But fewer than 15% of sales reps consistently send structured recaps after calls, according to data from Gong. The ones who do have 24% higher win rates. That's not a coincidence — it's the difference between a conversation and a commitment.

**Why it works:** A structured recap does three things at once. First, it proves you were listening — not performing, actually listening. Second, it creates mutual accountability. When you put "Sarah will send the security questionnaire by Thursday" in writing, Sarah sends the security questionnaire by Thursday. Third, it gives your champion something to forward internally. Your polished "great to connect" email gets deleted. A clear recap with next steps gets forwarded to the decision maker with "see below."

**Try this today:** After your next call, don't write a follow-up. Write a recap. Use this format: (1) Key discussion points — two or three bullets max. (2) Action items with owners and deadlines. (3) Proposed next step with a specific date. Send it within 10 minutes. Watch what happens.
