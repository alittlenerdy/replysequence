# The 10-Minute Rule

**Subject line:** The 10-minute window that 3x's your close rate

---

You just had a great discovery call. The prospect was nodding, asking questions, even dropped the "this is exactly what we need" line. You hang up, check Slack, grab coffee, knock out two other things, and three hours later you send a follow-up that reads like it was written by someone who vaguely remembers the conversation.

That gap killed your deal.

**The insight:** Reps who send follow-ups within 10 minutes of hanging up close deals at 3x the rate of reps who wait more than an hour. This isn't some made-up SaaS stat. It maps directly to the peak-end rule from behavioral psychology — people judge an experience based on its most intense moment and how it ended. Your call ended on a high. The follow-up IS the ending. Wait three hours and the ending becomes silence. Wait a day and you're already forgotten.

**Why it works:** The prospect's emotional state after a good call is the highest it will ever be. They're bought in. They're imagining the solution. Every minute you wait, that energy decays. A fast, specific follow-up locks in the momentum. It also signals something powerful: you're organized, you care, and you actually listened. Most reps don't send anything same-day. Showing up in their inbox while they're still thinking about the call is a competitive advantage.

**Try this today:** Set a hard rule — no follow-up leaves your outbox more than 10 minutes after a call ends. If writing a good email in 10 minutes sounds impossible, that's exactly why tools like ReplySequence exist. But even a rough, honest recap sent fast beats a polished email sent tomorrow.
