# Your CRM Is Lying to You

**Subject line:** Your CRM is lying to you (and your forecast is based on the lies)

---

Pull up your CRM right now. Pick any deal that's been in pipeline for more than two weeks. Read the notes. I'll wait.

If you're lucky, there's a one-liner like "good call, next steps discussed." If you're honest with yourself, half those deal records have no notes at all. And yet your pipeline review tomorrow is going to treat those numbers like gospel.

**The insight:** Salesforce's own research shows reps spend about 28% of their week actually selling. The rest is admin, internal meetings, and — supposedly — logging CRM data. But here's the dirty secret: they don't. A study from Introhive found that 40% of CRM data goes stale within a year, and reps only log about 30% of their customer interactions. Your pipeline isn't a pipeline. It's a guess built on incomplete notes from people who were too busy (or too lazy) to write things down. The deals you think are at 60% probability? Nobody actually knows because the last meaningful update was two weeks ago.

**Why it works (or rather, why it doesn't):** Manual data entry is a motivation problem, not a discipline problem. Reps don't skip notes because they're bad at their jobs. They skip notes because logging a call summary into Salesforce is the least rewarding task in their day. The only fix is to remove the human from the logging step entirely.

**Try this today:** Pick your three biggest open deals. Go read the CRM notes. If any of them have less than a paragraph of context from the last conversation, you've found the problem. Now imagine those notes were auto-generated from the call transcript — accurate, complete, and there before the rep even thinks about it.
